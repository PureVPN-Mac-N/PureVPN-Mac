## How a VPN Protects Your Mac

<a href="#">Mac PureVPN</a> protection works through encryption and IP masking:

### The Encrypted Tunnel

When you connect <a href="#">PureVPN Mac</a>, it creates an encrypted tunnel between your Mac and
a PureVPN server. All your internet traffic travels through this tunnel:

**Before the VPN**: Your traffic goes directly from your Mac to websites, visible to your ISP,
network administrators, and anyone monitoring the network. Your real IP address identifies you.

**With the VPN**: Your traffic is encrypted on your Mac, travels through the encrypted tunnel to
the PureVPN server, and only then reaches the internet. Anyone monitoring your local network sees
only encrypted data going to the VPN server — not what websites you visit or what data you send.

The website you visit sees the PureVPN server's IP address, not yours. Your real IP and location
are hidden behind the VPN server.

### What This Protects Against

<a href="#">PureVPN</a> encryption protects against several threats:

**Network eavesdropping**: On public Wi-Fi, others on the network cannot intercept your encrypted traffic.
**ISP tracking**: Your ISP sees encrypted traffic to the VPN, not your browsing.
**IP-based tracking**: Websites and trackers see the VPN IP, not your identifying real IP.
**Location tracking**: Your real geographic location is hidden behind the VPN server's location.

## Configuring the Kill Switch

<a href="#">PureVPN Mac</a> kill switch configuration is essential for guaranteed privacy:

### Why the Kill Switch Matters

The kill switch addresses a specific privacy vulnerability: VPN connections can occasionally drop
due to network issues, server problems, or protocol interruptions. Without protection, a dropped
VPN connection means your Mac silently reverts to the unprotected direct connection — exposing your
real IP and unencrypted traffic until you notice and reconnect.

<a href="#">PureVPN</a> kill switch eliminates this risk by monitoring the VPN connection and
immediately blocking all internet traffic if the VPN drops. Your traffic is only ever transmitted
when the VPN is active — if the VPN is not protecting your traffic, no traffic goes out at all.

### When to Use the Kill Switch

Enable the <a href="#">PureVPN Mac app</a> kill switch when guaranteed privacy is important:

Handling sensitive data where any exposure is unacceptable. P2P file sharing where IP exposure
should be prevented. Any situation where you need certainty that your real IP is never revealed,
even momentarily.

## Split Tunneling Configuration

<a href="#">PureVPN Mac</a> split tunneling provides granular control over which traffic uses the VPN:

### How Split Tunneling Works

By default, a VPN routes all your Mac's traffic through the encrypted tunnel. Split tunneling lets
you exclude specific applications from the VPN, letting them use the direct connection while
everything else stays protected:

**VPN-routed applications**: Applications you want protected — web browsers, email, messaging —
route through the <a href="#">PureVPN</a> encrypted tunnel.

**Direct-connection applications**: Applications you want on the direct connection — perhaps a
local network application, a service that blocks VPN connections, or a bandwidth-intensive
download where you want full local speed — bypass the VPN.

This selective routing balances privacy protection with the practical needs of applications that
work better on a direct connection.
